How to Start a Petty Cash Box

No cash box yet? No process? No problem. Set up petty cash properly from day one and avoid the messy cleanup later.

Starting a petty cash box means choosing a float amount, defining who has access, setting clear rules, and tracking every transaction from the first day.

Step 1: Decide the Starting Float

The float is the amount of cash you put into the box on day one. It should cover 2–4 weeks of expected small cash expenses.

  • Small office (5–10 people): $200–$500
  • Retail shop or workshop: $500–$1,000
  • Field team or construction site: $500–$2,000

Start lower than you think you need. You can always increase the float after the first month once you see actual spending patterns. It's easier to add more than to explain why there's too much cash sitting in a box.

Step 2: Choose a Custodian

One person manages the box. Not two, not "whoever is around." One person is accountable for the physical cash, the log, and the receipts.

Typically this is an office manager, a team lead, or the business owner themselves. The custodian should be the only person with direct access to the cash. Others request cash from them, and every disbursement gets documented.

Step 3: Set the Rules

Write them down. Even if your team is small. Especially if your team is small.

  • Maximum per transaction — e.g., no single disbursement over $50 without manager approval
  • Allowed categories — office supplies, postage, minor repairs, meals. Not personal expenses, salary advances, or entertainment
  • Receipt requirement — every disbursement needs a receipt or voucher
  • Reconciliation schedule — weekly or biweekly at minimum
  • Replenishment threshold — request a top-up when the box drops below 25% of the float

Need a ready-made template? See the petty cash policy template.

Step 4: Start Tracking from Day One

The biggest mistake is starting the cash box but waiting to track it. The first $50 goes out undocumented, then the second, and by the end of the month you have $200 missing with no trail.

Log the very first transaction. Whether it's a paper petty cash log, a spreadsheet, or a digital tracker — the system starts working when you start using it.

Common Mistakes When Starting

  • No written rules — "everyone knows" turns into "nobody agrees" after the first discrepancy
  • Too much cash in the box — more cash = higher risk, and if you don't spend it, it just sits there doing nothing
  • Multiple people with access — if everyone can reach into the box, nobody is accountable
  • No receipts from the start — retroactive documentation doesn't work; undocumented disbursements are unverifiable
  • Never reconciling — the box should be reconciled regularly, not just when problems appear

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money should I start with in a petty cash box?

Start with an amount that covers 2–4 weeks of expected small cash expenses. For a small office, $200–$500 is typical. For a retail shop or field team, $500–$1,000 may be needed. Track your spending for the first month and adjust the float based on actual usage.

Who should manage the petty cash box?

Assign one person as the custodian — typically an office manager, team lead, or trusted employee. This person is responsible for the physical cash, logging transactions, and reconciling the balance. Having one custodian creates clear accountability.

Do I need a physical lock box?

Yes. Keep petty cash in a secure, lockable container — a metal cash box, a locking drawer, or a small safe. The custodian should be the only person with the key. Leaving cash in an unlocked drawer or envelope is asking for problems.

What rules should I set for petty cash?

At minimum: set a maximum amount per transaction (e.g., $50), require a receipt for every disbursement, define what expenses are allowed (supplies, postage, meals — not personal items), and require the custodian to reconcile weekly. Write the rules down and share them with anyone who handles cash.

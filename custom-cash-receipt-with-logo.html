Why Brand Your Cash Receipts?

You hand a client a plain receipt with just an amount and a date. It works, but it looks like it came from a calculator roll. Now imagine the same receipt with your business logo at the top, your business name, and a clean layout. Same information — completely different impression.

A branded receipt says "I'm a real business." It builds trust, especially when you're working with new clients who don't know you yet. It's the difference between a scribbled note and a professional document.

And it costs nothing extra. Upload your logo once in SpendNote, and every receipt from that cash box automatically includes it.

How to Add Your Logo

Step 1: Open SpendNote and go to User Settings.

Step 2: Upload your default logo (PNG, JPG, or WebP). Square or horizontal logos work best.

Step 3: Save. From now on, every receipt will include your logo automatically — printed, emailed, or downloaded as PDF.

Pro tip: On the Pro plan, you can also set a different logo per cash box. Perfect if you run multiple businesses or departments under one account.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I add my business logo to cash receipts?

Yes. In SpendNote, go to User Settings and upload your default logo. It will appear on every receipt — printed, emailed, or downloaded as PDF. On the Pro plan, you can also set a different logo per cash box.

What logo formats are supported?

SpendNote accepts PNG, JPG, and WebP logo files. For best results, use a square or horizontal logo with a transparent background. The logo will be resized automatically to fit the receipt layout.

Can I have different logos for different cash boxes?

Yes, on the Pro plan. Your default logo is set in User Settings and applies to all receipts. With Pro, each cash box can have its own logo — perfect if you run multiple businesses or departments.

Is the custom receipt a tax invoice?

No. Even with a logo, a SpendNote receipt is proof that cash was received. It is not a tax invoice, official accounting record, or legally binding document. Adding your logo makes the receipt look professional but does not change its purpose.

Why Tutors Need Receipts for Cash Sessions

You tutor the Garcia kids every Monday and Wednesday. Mom pays you $60 in cash after each session. After three weeks, she hands you $60 and says, "This covers last Wednesday too." Did she already pay for that one?

Without a receipt, you're both guessing. With a receipt, you check your phone: Wednesday March 3, $60, Garcia family. Paid. No awkwardness, no confusion.

It works both ways. Parents know they won't be charged twice, and you know you won't miss a payment. Everyone has the same record.

What Your Tutoring Receipt Should Include

  • Amount received — The exact cash you were paid
  • Student or family name — Who paid you
  • Date — When the payment happened
  • Session note — What the payment was for (e.g., "Math tutoring 1 hr")
  • Your name — Who received the cash

SpendNote fills in date, time, and receipt number automatically. You just enter the amount, pick the student's family, and optionally add a note about the session. Done in under 30 seconds.

How It Works

Step 1: Open SpendNote on your phone after the family pays you.

Step 2: Tap "IN" (you're receiving cash). Enter the amount and select or add the family as a contact.

Step 3: Done. The receipt is generated instantly. Email it to the parents, or keep it as your own record. Next session, the family is already saved — even faster.

If you tutor multiple students, each family is saved as a separate contact. You can pull up the complete payment history for any student in seconds — perfect for tracking how many sessions have been paid.

This Is Not a Tax Document

Let's be clear about what SpendNote does and doesn't do:

  • It does: Record that you received cash from a specific family on a specific date for a tutoring session
  • It does not: Generate invoices, calculate taxes, or create official accounting records

If you need to report tutoring income, or if parents want to claim education expenses for tax purposes, that's a separate process. SpendNote just makes sure neither side forgets what was paid and when.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should a tutor give receipts for cash payments?

Yes. A receipt protects both the tutor and the family. Parents have proof they paid, and you have a clear record of your income. It also looks professional and avoids any confusion about missed or duplicate payments.

Is a tutoring cash receipt a tax document?

No. A SpendNote receipt is simply proof that cash was received for a tutoring session. It is not a tax document, invoice, or official accounting record. If you need to report tutoring income for tax purposes, consult a tax professional.

Can I track sessions and payments from multiple students?

Yes. SpendNote lets you save each student or family as a contact. Every payment is logged with the name, amount, and date. You can filter your history by student to see exactly how many sessions they have paid for.

Does this work for music teachers, language tutors, and other private instructors?

Absolutely. Any private instructor who receives cash payments can use SpendNote. Piano teachers, language tutors, SAT prep coaches, art instructors — the receipt works the same way regardless of the subject.

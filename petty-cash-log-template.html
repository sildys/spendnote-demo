What is a Petty Cash Log?

A petty cash log (also called a petty cash book or petty cash register) is a record-keeping document that tracks all small cash disbursements made from your office's petty cash fund. It helps teams maintain accountability, reconcile balances, and ensure every dollar is properly documented for audit compliance.

How to Use a Petty Cash Log Template

1

Set Starting Balance

Record your initial petty cash fund amount (e.g., $200 office float). This is your opening balance.

2

Log Each Disbursement

When cash is given to an employee, record: date, recipient name, amount, expense category, and have them sign a petty cash voucher.

3

Attach Receipt Proof

Staple or scan the receipt to verify the expense (e.g., office supplies receipt for $15.47). This maintains your audit trail.

4

Calculate Running Balance

Subtract each disbursement from the previous balance to track remaining petty cash. Digital logs do this automatically.

5

Reconcile Regularly

Count physical cash and compare to your log balance. Investigate any discrepancies immediately and document the reconciliation.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should be included in a petty cash log?

Every petty cash log entry should include: transaction date, recipient/employee name, disbursement amount, expense category (e.g., office supplies, team lunch), and a reference to the attached receipt. A running balance helps track remaining petty cash at all times.

How often should I reconcile my petty cash log?

Most offices reconcile petty cash monthly, but high-volume teams may benefit from weekly reconciliation to catch discrepancies early and maintain accurate records.

Can I use a petty cash log for employee reimbursements?

Yes! A petty cash log is ideal for tracking small employee reimbursements (typically under $50) for out-of-pocket expenses like parking, postage, or office supplies.

What's the difference between a petty cash log and a petty cash voucher?

A petty cash voucher is a single-transaction receipt signed by the employee receiving cash. A petty cash log is the master record tracking all vouchers and maintaining the running balance of your office petty cash fund.

Do I need special software for petty cash tracking?

Not necessarily. You can start with a paper log or Excel template. However, digital petty cash software like SpendNote eliminates manual calculations, provides automatic reconciliation, and offers cloud backup for better internal controls.

How much should I keep in my office petty cash fund?

Most small offices maintain $100-$300 in petty cash. The amount depends on your typical monthly miscellaneous expenses. Review your petty cash log quarterly to adjust the fund size as needed.

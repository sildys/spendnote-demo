Why Handymen Need Receipts for Cash Jobs

You fix a leaky faucet, patch drywall, hang shelves. The homeowner hands you $350 in cash. A week later, they call back with another job and ask: "Did I pay you for last time?" Without a receipt, you're guessing.

With a receipt, there's no guessing. Every job, every payment, every client — documented. You look professional, and the client has proof they paid.

It protects both sides. The client knows exactly what they paid and when. You know exactly who owes you and who doesn't.

Who This Is For

Solo Handymen

You do jobs for multiple homeowners each week. Cash at the door after every job. A receipt for each one means you never lose track of who paid what.

Handyman Services with a Small Team

You have 2-3 workers doing jobs across town. When they collect cash on your behalf, a receipt keeps everyone accountable. You know what was collected, by whom, from which client.

Property Maintenance Workers

Regular maintenance for rental properties or small businesses. Monthly or weekly cash payments. A receipt trail means you and the property owner always agree on what's been paid.

What Your Handyman Receipt Should Include

  • Amount received — The exact cash paid for the job
  • Client name — Who paid you
  • Date — When the payment happened
  • Job note — What the payment was for (e.g., "Kitchen faucet repair")
  • Your name or business name — Who received the cash

SpendNote fills in date, time, and receipt number automatically. You just enter the amount, pick the client, and optionally add a job note. Done in under 30 seconds.

How It Works

Step 1: Open SpendNote on your phone after the client pays you.

Step 2: Tap "IN" (you're receiving cash). Enter the amount and select or add the client as a contact.

Step 3: Done. The receipt is generated instantly. Print it, email it to the client, or download as PDF. Next job for the same client is even faster — their name is already saved.

If you work for multiple clients, each one is saved as a separate contact. You can pull up the complete payment history for any client in seconds.

This Is Not an Invoice

Let's be clear about what SpendNote does and doesn't do:

  • It does: Record that you received cash from a specific client on a specific date for a specific job
  • It does not: Generate invoices, calculate taxes, or create official accounting records

If you need to issue invoices or report handyman income for taxes, that's a separate process. SpendNote just makes sure neither you nor the client forgets what was paid and when.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should a handyman give receipts for cash jobs?

Yes. A receipt protects both you and the client. The client has proof they paid, and you have proof you were paid. It builds trust, prevents disputes about amounts, and makes you look professional.

Is a handyman cash receipt the same as an invoice?

No. A SpendNote receipt is proof that cash was received for a job. It is not an invoice, tax document, or official accounting record. If you need to issue invoices for tax purposes, that is a separate process.

Can I track payments from multiple clients?

Yes. SpendNote lets you save each client as a contact. Every payment is logged with the client name, amount, and date. You can search your full history by client, date, or amount at any time.

How fast can I create a receipt after a job?

Under 30 seconds. Open SpendNote, tap IN, enter the amount, pick the client, and you are done. The receipt is generated instantly and can be printed, emailed, or downloaded as PDF.

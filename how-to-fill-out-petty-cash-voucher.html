How to Fill Out a Petty Cash Voucher

Step-by-step guide with field explanations, signature requirements, and digital alternatives

What is a Petty Cash Voucher?

A petty cash voucher is a document that records small cash transactions. It serves as proof that money was given to or received from someone, creating an audit trail for your petty cash fund.

Every time cash leaves or enters your petty cash box, you should fill out a voucher. This keeps your records accurate and helps prevent discrepancies during reconciliation.

Required Fields on a Petty Cash Voucher

A complete petty cash voucher must include the following information:

1. Date

What to write: The date when the cash transaction occurs (not when you're filling out the form later).

Why it matters: Dates help you track cash flow over time and match vouchers to your cash log or ledger.

2. Amount

What to write: The exact cash amount, written in both numbers and words.

Example: $45.00 (Forty-five dollars)

Why it matters: Writing the amount twice prevents alterations and reduces errors.

3. Purpose / Description

What to write: A clear, specific reason for the cash disbursement.

Good examples:

  • "Office supplies – printer paper and pens"
  • "Taxi fare for client meeting"
  • "Postage stamps for invoices"

Bad examples: "Misc", "Stuff", "Expenses" (too vague)

4. Recipient Name and Contact

What to write: Full name of the person receiving the cash, plus their email or phone number.

Why it matters: This creates accountability and makes it easy to follow up if there are questions about the transaction.

5. Signatures

Who signs: Both the person giving the cash (custodian) and the person receiving it (recipient).

Why it matters: Dual signatures confirm that both parties agree on the amount and purpose, reducing disputes.

Step-by-Step: Filling Out a Voucher

  1. Write today's date in the date field.
  2. Enter the cash amount in numbers, then write it out in words.
  3. Describe the purpose clearly and specifically.
  4. Record recipient details (name, email, or phone).
  5. Get both signatures before handing over the cash.
  6. File the voucher with your petty cash log or attach it to your cash box records.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Missing signatures: Always get both parties to sign before the transaction is complete.
  • Vague descriptions: "Miscellaneous" doesn't help during audits. Be specific.
  • Incorrect amounts: Double-check numbers before signing.
  • No date: Always record the transaction date, not the date you're catching up on paperwork.

Why Proper Voucher Completion Matters

Filling out petty cash vouchers correctly helps you:

  • Maintain accurate records: Every transaction is documented with full details.
  • Pass audits: Clear vouchers with signatures satisfy internal and external auditors.
  • Prevent fraud: Dual signatures and detailed descriptions reduce the risk of unauthorized cash use.
  • Reconcile faster: Complete vouchers make it easy to match cash on hand with your log.

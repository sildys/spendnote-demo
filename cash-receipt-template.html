The Problem: Cash Moves, But Nothing Is Documented

Someone pays you in cash. You pay someone else. Money changes hands five times a week — and none of it is documented.

Then the questions start: "Where did that $200 go?" "Did we already pay the courier?" "Who took cash from the box on Thursday?"

The answer is always the same: nobody knows, because there's no receipt.

What a Cash Receipt Needs to Be Useful

A receipt isn't useful because it exists — it's useful because it answers questions later. Here's what it needs:

Receipt Number

Sequential ID for tracking and reference. SpendNote generates these automatically.

Date & Time

When the cash changed hands. Required for any dispute resolution or audit trail.

Payer & Payee

Who gave the cash and who received it. Full names, not just initials.

Amount

Exact amount in figures. SpendNote supports any currency and line-item breakdowns.

Purpose / Description

What the cash was for. One line is enough — but it needs to be there.

Signature Lines

Both parties sign. This is what makes the receipt legally useful as evidence.

Why Most People Don't Issue Receipts (And Why It Costs Them)

It's not laziness. It's friction:

  • The Word template is somewhere on the shared drive — but nobody can find it when they need it
  • Nobody remembers the last receipt number — so they guess, or skip it
  • It takes too long — by the time you open the file, format it, and print it, the moment has passed
  • "I'll do it later" — and later never comes

The result: cash moves without a trace. And when something doesn't add up, there's nothing to go back to.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I prove a cash payment was made?
Generate a cash receipt at the moment the payment happens. It should include the amount, date, who paid, who received, and why. Both parties keep a copy. SpendNote generates this in about 30 seconds — print, PDF, or email.

What happens if I don't issue a receipt for cash?
Without a receipt, there is no proof the payment happened. If there's a dispute, you have no documentation. If you need to reconcile your cash box, you'll have an unexplained gap. Receipts take 30 seconds — not having one can cost you hours.

Why not just use a Word template?
Templates work until you lose a copy, forget to number a receipt, or need to find a transaction from last month. SpendNote auto-numbers receipts, stores the full history, and lets you search, filter, and export at any time.

Can I use this for petty cash?
Yes. SpendNote supports petty cash workflows with two-copy print receipts, so both the person paying out and the person receiving cash have a signed record.

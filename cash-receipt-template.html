What a Cash Receipt Template Needs

A cash receipt is proof that a cash payment was made. Whether it's a petty cash reimbursement, a customer purchase, or an internal transfer, the receipt needs the same core fields.

Receipt Number

Sequential ID for tracking and reference. SpendNote generates these automatically.

Date & Time

When the cash changed hands. Required for any dispute resolution or audit trail.

Payer & Payee

Who gave the cash and who received it. Full names, not just initials.

Amount

Exact amount in figures. SpendNote supports any currency and line-item breakdowns.

Purpose / Description

What the cash was for. One line is enough — but it needs to be there.

Signature Lines

Both parties sign. This is what makes the receipt legally useful as evidence.

How to Fill Out a Cash Receipt Template

If you use a Word or PDF template, here's what filling one out actually requires — and what SpendNote handles automatically:

  1. Assign a receipt number: With a template, you track this manually. SpendNote auto-generates sequential receipt numbers.
  2. Write the date and time: Easy to forget or misread on paper. SpendNote timestamps every transaction at creation.
  3. Fill in payer and payee: Two name fields, typed by hand. SpendNote pulls from your saved contacts or lets you type once.
  4. Enter the amount: One field in a template. SpendNote formats it, supports any currency, and stores it in your history.
  5. Describe the transaction: A blank line on most templates. SpendNote keeps this searchable across your full history.
  6. Sign and distribute: With a template, you print, sign, and hand out copies. SpendNote prints two copies in one click — or emails both parties instantly.

When to Use a Printable Cash Receipt

Any time cash changes hands and you need a record. The most common situations:

  • Petty cash reimbursements — Employee paid out of pocket, gets reimbursed from the cash box
  • Cash sales — Customer pays cash, needs proof of purchase
  • Internal transfers — Cash moving between registers, locations, or shifts
  • Cash advances — Employee receives cash for a business expense before the trip
  • Deposits and collections — Cash collected from events, fees, or dues
  • Rent payments — Landlords issuing an acknowledgement of payment for cash rent

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a cash receipt need to be signed?
For internal use (petty cash, advances, handoffs), yes. A signature from both parties is what gives the receipt its value as evidence. For customer sales, a signature is optional but recommended for high-value transactions.

What's the difference between a cash receipt and an invoice?
An invoice is a request for payment. A cash receipt is proof that payment was made. You issue an invoice before payment; you issue a receipt after.

Can I add a company logo to the receipt?
Yes. SpendNote supports custom logos on all receipt formats — print, PDF, and email.

How many copies should I print?
Two: one for the person who gave the cash, one for the person who received it. SpendNote's default print layout is a two-copy format on a single page.

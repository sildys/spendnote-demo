Petty Cash Voucher Sample

Real examples of completed petty cash vouchers for common business expenses

What Makes a Good Petty Cash Voucher Sample?

A good sample voucher shows you exactly what information to include and how to format it. Below are real-world examples for typical petty cash transactions, demonstrating proper completion with all required fields.

Sample 1: Office Supplies Purchase

Petty Cash Voucher

Date: March 4, 2026
Amount: $32.50 (Thirty-two dollars and fifty cents)
Purpose: Office supplies – printer paper (2 reams), sticky notes, and ballpoint pens
Recipient: Sarah Johnson (sarah.j@company.com)
Custodian Signature: M. Chen
Recipient Signature: S. Johnson
Receipt Attached: Yes

Why this works: The description is specific (lists exactly what was purchased), the amount is written in both formats, and both parties signed. The original receipt is attached for verification.

Sample 2: Travel Expense Reimbursement

Petty Cash Voucher

Date: March 3, 2026
Amount: $45.00 (Forty-five dollars)
Purpose: Taxi fare for client meeting at downtown office (round trip)
Recipient: David Lee (david.lee@company.com)
Custodian Signature: M. Chen
Recipient Signature: D. Lee
Receipt Attached: Yes (taxi receipt)

Why this works: The purpose explains the business reason (client meeting) and specifies round trip, making it clear why the amount is justified.

Sample 3: Postage and Shipping

Petty Cash Voucher

Date: March 2, 2026
Amount: $18.75 (Eighteen dollars and seventy-five cents)
Purpose: Postage stamps for mailing invoices to clients (25 stamps)
Recipient: Emily Rodriguez (emily.r@company.com)
Custodian Signature: M. Chen
Recipient Signature: E. Rodriguez
Receipt Attached: Yes (post office receipt)

Why this works: The voucher specifies the quantity (25 stamps) and the business purpose (mailing invoices), providing a clear audit trail.

Key Elements in Every Sample

Notice that all three samples include:

  • Exact date of the transaction
  • Amount in two formats (numbers and words)
  • Specific description with item details or business justification
  • Recipient name and contact for accountability
  • Dual signatures from custodian and recipient
  • Receipt attachment when applicable

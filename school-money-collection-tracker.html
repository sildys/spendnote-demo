The Class Fund Problem

You're the class treasurer. Twenty-three families owe $25 each for the school trip. Parents hand you cash at pickup, during events, or send it with their kids. After two weeks, you've collected a stack of bills and a growing headache: who paid? Who didn't? Where's the list?

A paper sign-up sheet gets lost. A group chat message gets buried. And the parent who insists they already paid? You have no way to prove or disprove it.

SpendNote fixes this. Every cash payment gets logged with the family name, amount, and date. You can show the full list to anyone who asks. No disputes, no guessing.

How It Works for Group Collections

Step 1: Create a cash box for the collection (e.g., "5th Grade Field Trip Fund").

Step 2: When a family pays, tap "IN", enter the amount, and select the family as a contact. Done in seconds.

Step 3: The receipt is generated instantly. Email it to the parent or keep it as your own record. Your dashboard shows the running total and who has paid.

When you spend money from the fund (supplies, tickets, deposits), log it as "OUT". The balance updates automatically. At the end, you have a complete in-and-out history to present to the group.

Need to show the group a summary? Export a filtered report for any time period or any specific collection.

This Is Not an Accounting Tool

Let's be clear about what SpendNote does and doesn't do:

  • It does: Track who paid into the fund, how much, and when — and who spent from it
  • It does not: Generate tax reports, handle bank accounts, or produce official financial statements

If your school or PTA requires formal accounting, that's a separate process. SpendNote simply gives you the transparency that parents want: proof of who paid, what was spent, and what's left.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I track who has paid into the class fund?

Save each family as a contact in SpendNote. When they hand you cash, log the payment with their name. Your transaction list shows exactly who has paid and when. Anyone not on the list has not paid yet.

Can I give parents a receipt for their class fund contribution?

Yes. Every payment you log in SpendNote generates a receipt automatically. You can email it to the parent, print it, or download it as PDF. Parents appreciate having proof of their contribution.

Can I track spending from the class fund too?

Yes. Use IN for money collected and OUT for money spent. SpendNote shows the running balance so you always know how much is left in the fund. You can show the full history to parents at any time.

Is SpendNote an accounting tool for schools?

No. SpendNote is for internal cash tracking and receipts. It is not an accounting, invoicing, or tax tool. It simply documents who paid what and where the money went — perfect for class fund transparency.
